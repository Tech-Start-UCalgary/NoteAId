The team found greater playoff success, qualifying for the Stanley Cup playoffs for their first five years after moving to Calgary. Led by Kent Nilsson's 49-goal, 131-point season, the Flames qualified for the playoffs in their first season in Calgary with a 39–27–14 record, good for third in the Patrick Division. [15] This early success was not soon repeated. [10] The Saddledome hosted the NHL All-Star Game in 1985, a 6–4 victory by the Wales Conference. From 1984–85 to 1990–91, the Flames tallied 90 points in every season but one. Montreal rookie goaltender Patrick Roy was nearly unbeatable in the last two games, allowing only four goals en route to winning the Conn Smythe Trophy.
